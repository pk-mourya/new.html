Flipkart is an e-commerce company in India that sells a variety of products, including books, movies, and mobile phones. It was founded in 2007 by Sachin and Binny Bansal, who were both former Amazon employees. How it started: Flipkart started as an online bookstore in a two-bedroom apartment in Bengaluru The company's initial investment came from the founders' families Flipkart grew rapidly, receiving 100 orders per day by 2008 What it offers: Flipkart offers over 80 million products across more than 80 categories Flipkart is known for pioneering services such as Cash on Delivery, No Cost EMI, and easy returns How it has grown: In 2010, Flipkart acquired WeRead, which helped it build its foundational strength in digital retail of books In 2018, American retailer Walmart paid USD 16 billion to buy out Flipkart What its goals are: Flipkart aims to democratize commerce in India, drive access and affordability, and delight customers Flipkart has pledged to completely transition to Electric Vehicles (EVs) by 2030 Flipkart - Wikipedia Flipkart was founded in October 2007 in Bangalore by Sachin Bansal and Binny Bansal, alumni of the IIT Delhi and former Amazon emp...
